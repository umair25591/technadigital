## **Founder Coaching & Advisory Services**

Coaching for founders at Spearpoint Advisors isn't a weekly check-in where you talk about your goals. It's a structured engagement built around the specific challenges you face as the owner of a growing, founder-led business.

Depending on where you are and where you're going, our founder coaching and advisory work typically covers:

**Getting out of the day-to-day** — Building the systems, team, and accountability structures that let you step back from operations without everything falling apart.

**Leadership development** — Becoming the leader your business needs at this stage — not the one who built it from scratch. Delegation, decision-making, and building a team that owns outcomes.

**Business health and value** — Understanding what your business is actually worth today, what's driving the number, and what would move it higher. Most founders are sitting on more — or less — than they think.

**Exit and transition planning** — Whether you're thinking about selling in three years or fifteen, startup founder coaching always includes a conversation about the end game — because how you build now determines what your options look like later.

**Owner readiness** — What do you actually want your life to look like on the other side of this business? Most founders have never been asked that question seriously. We ask it early, and we build around the answer.

**Peer accountability — CFO Circles** — For founders who want more than 1:1 coaching, our CFO Circles peer advisory groups bring together business owners across the Pacific Northwest for shared accountability, honest conversation, and the kind of perspective you can't get from inside your own company.

## **You're the Founder. You're Also the Ceiling.**

Most founders reach a point where the business can't grow faster than they can personally manage it. Every decision runs through you. Every crisis lands on your desk. Every hire needs your approval, every client wants your attention, and every problem eventually becomes yours to solve.

That's not a business problem. That's an owner dependency problem — and it's one of the most common and most costly patterns in founder-led businesses.

The hard truth is this: a business that can't run without you isn't really an asset. It's a job. And the longer it stays that way, the harder it becomes to grow it, step back from it, or eventually exit it on your terms.

"You didn't build this business to be trapped in it. Founder coaching helps you get out of the way — so the business can become what it was always capable of being."

## **The Forces That End Businesses Don't Send a Warning**

50% of business owners are forced out of their business by one of the 5 Ds — **Death · Divorce · Disability · Disagreement · Disruption** — not by choice, not on their timeline. And for most founders, 80% of their personal net worth is tied up in the business that just became someone else's problem to sort out.

Founders are the most exposed to this risk of anyone. When the entire business runs through one person — their relationships, their knowledge, their daily presence — a single unexpected event can erase years of value almost overnight. The business doesn't just struggle. It can collapse.

This is why founder coaching at Spearpoint is never just about growth or leadership. It's always connected to protection. Building a business that can run without you isn't just a strategic goal. It's the single most important thing you can do to protect what you've built — for yourself, for your family, and for the people who depend on your business being around.

"50% of owners are forced to exit. 80% had no plan. Don't be in that 80%."
