## What Makes CEO Coaching Different from General Executive Coaching

Most executive coaching focuses on individual behavior — communication style, emotional intelligence, personal effectiveness. Those things matter. But for a CEO, the stakes are higher and the scope is broader. The way a CEO leads doesn't just affect their performance. It affects the performance, culture, and valuation of the entire business.

#### **The CEO Is the Culture**

Every organization takes on the character of its leader. The way a CEO handles conflict, makes decisions, holds people accountable, and communicates under pressure becomes the operating norm for the entire company. CEO coaching at Spearpoint addresses this directly — not just how you lead in a coaching session, but how your leadership shows up on the floor, in the boardroom, and in the numbers.

#### **The CEO's Blind Spots Are the Company's Blind Spots**

The higher you are in an organization, the less unfiltered feedback you receive. People tell CEOs what they think the CEO wants to hear. Problems get softened before they reach the top. CEO coaching creates a structured space for honest assessment — of your leadership, your team, your organizational design, and the gaps between where you are and where the business needs you to be.

#### **The CEO's Exit Is the Company's Exit**

For most founder-led businesses, the CEO's personal goals and the company's transition readiness are inseparable. If the CEO isn't ready — emotionally, financially, operationally — the business isn't ready. CEO leadership coaching at Spearpoint addresses both dimensions simultaneously, building the leader and the business in parallel.

## What the C-Suite Needs from Its CEO

#### **Building a Leadership Team That Owns Outcomes**

A CEO is only as strong as the team they build around them. Most founder-led businesses have a capable owner and a thin leadership bench — and that gap is one of the most common and most costly patterns we see. Executive coaching for CEOs at Spearpoint always includes work on the team: who's in the right seats, who needs to be developed, who needs to be replaced, and how to build a culture of genuine accountability across the C-suite.

#### **Setting the Right Cadence for the Leadership Team**

High-performing C-suites operate with a clear rhythm — regular strategic reviews, structured accountability meetings, defined decision rights, and communication norms that keep the leadership team aligned without pulling the CEO into every conversation. We help CEOs design and install that cadence, so the organization runs on a system rather than on the CEO's personal bandwidth.

#### **Separating the CEO Role from the Founder Identity**

One of the most common and least discussed challenges in CEO coaching for founder-led businesses is the identity challenge. When you built the business, you were the business. Stepping into a true CEO role — one that operates through a leadership team rather than through personal execution — requires a fundamental shift in how you see yourself and how you spend your time. We work through that transition deliberately, because the businesses that scale are the ones whose CEOs make it.

#### **Preparing the C-Suite for Leadership Transition**

Whether the CEO is preparing to step back, bring on a successor, or position the business for sale, the readiness of the C-suite determines whether the transition succeeds. We work with CEOs and their leadership teams to build the depth, documentation, and decision-making independence that allows the business to perform through a transition — not just before it.

## 50% of Owners Are Forced Out. Most Never Saw It Coming.

Death. Divorce. Disability. Disagreement. Disruption. These are the 5 Ds — and they account for half of all business exits. Not planned. Not on the owner's terms. And 80% of those owners had no plan in place when it happened, despite 80% of their personal net worth being tied up in the business they just lost control of.

**Death · Divorce · Disability · Disagreement · Disruption**

For CEOs, the risk is compounded. CEO coaching that is connected to exit readiness isn't a luxury. It's the most important protection a CEO can build into the business.

"The 5 Ds don't announce themselves. The owners who are protected when they arrive are the ones who started preparing before they needed to."
