Successful on Paper. Exhausted in Reality.

Small business owners doing $2M–$150M in revenue have solved most of the problems that kill businesses in the early years. They have customers. They have a team. They have revenue. What they're still working through is the transition from running a business to actually owning one — a business that works for them rather than the other way around.

The day-to-day is relentless. Every decision, every client escalation, every hiring crisis eventually lands on the owner's desk. The team is capable — but not quite capable enough to run things without constant direction. And the business, as valuable as it feels, is almost entirely dependent on the owner's continued presence.

"The small business owners who get the best outcomes are the ones who started building intentionally before they felt the urgency."

What Makes Small Business Coaching Different at This Stage

Small business owners in the $2M–$150M range face a specific set of challenges that are different from the problems of a startup and different from the problems of a large corporation. The coaching and consulting work that helps them has to be built around those specific challenges — not adapted from a framework designed for a different kind of business.

The Transition from Operator to Owner

The single most important shift a small business owner can make is the shift from being the best operator in the business to being the architect of a business that operates without them. This transition is harder than it sounds. It requires letting go of the work you're best at, trusting people to do things differently than you would, and building systems that carry the institutional knowledge currently living in your head. Small business coaching that doesn't address this transition directly isn't addressing the real problem.

The Gap Between Revenue and Wealth

Many small business owners have built significant revenue and very little transferable wealth. The business generates income — but if it stopped tomorrow, or if the owner couldn't show up, the income would stop with it. The goal of small business consulting at Spearpoint is to close that gap: to turn a business that generates revenue into an asset that holds and grows value independently of the owner's daily presence.

The Cost of Doing Everything Yourself

At the $2M–$5M stage, doing everything yourself is often a necessity. At $5M–$15M, it's a habit. At $15M–$150M, it's a liability. The owners who scale successfully are the ones who recognize when the thing that got them here is the thing that's keeping them from getting to the next level — and who have the structure and support to make the transition deliberately rather than reactively.

The Planning Problem

Most small business owners have never written down what they actually want. Not for the business — for themselves. What does the ideal outcome look like? What does the business need to be worth for that outcome to be achievable? How many years do they have, and what would need to change in the business to make the timeline work? Small business coaching at Spearpoint starts with these questions — because a plan that isn't connected to what the owner actually wants isn't a plan worth following.

The Isolation Problem

Running a small business is one of the loneliest leadership positions there is. There is no board to challenge your thinking. There are no peers at your level to compare notes with. The people closest to you — your team, your family — have a stake in the decisions you make, which makes genuinely honest conversation rare. Small business coaching and peer advisory work addresses this directly, creating the outside perspective and accountability that most small business owners have never had access to.

What the $2M–$150M Stage Actually Requires

From Informal to Intentional

Businesses that reach $2M typically got there through hustle, relationships, and the owner's personal drive. What it takes to get from $2M to $10M — and from $10M to $50M — is fundamentally different. It requires documented processes, a real leadership team, financial discipline, and systems that don't depend on any one person to function. Small business consulting at Spearpoint is built around making that transition deliberately — not waiting for growth to expose the gaps.

From Single-Owner Decision-Making to Distributed Leadership

One of the most common patterns in small businesses at this stage is a capable team surrounding an owner who can't stop making all the decisions. The team waits. The owner decides. The business moves at the speed of one person's bandwidth — and that bandwidth has a ceiling. Building distributed decision-making isn't just an operational upgrade. It's one of the most significant value drivers in a small business, because it's what allows the business to grow and eventually transfer without the owner at the center of everything.

From Revenue Dependence to Asset Value

A small business that generates $2M in revenue but can't run without its owner isn't worth $2M. It's worth significantly less — because a buyer isn't buying revenue, they're buying a business that will continue to generate revenue after they take over. The gap between what a small business owner believes their company is worth and what a buyer will actually pay is almost always explained by owner dependency, customer concentration, and the absence of documented systems. Small business coaching addresses all three.

50% of Owners Are Forced Out. Most Never Saw It Coming.

Death. Divorce. Disability. Disagreement. Disruption. These are the 5 Ds — and they account for half of all business exits. Not planned. Not on the owner's terms. And 80% of those owners had no plan in place when it happened, despite 80% of their personal net worth being tied up in the business they just lost control of.

Death · Divorce · Disability · Disagreement · Disruption

For small business owners, the 5 Ds risk is especially acute. When the entire business runs through one person — their relationships, their knowledge, their daily decisions — a single unexpected event can erase years of value almost overnight. The business doesn't just struggle. It can collapse. Small business coaching that is connected to exit readiness and owner protection isn't a luxury. It's the most important investment a small business owner can make.

"The 5 Ds don't announce themselves. The owners who are protected when they arrive are the ones who started preparing before they needed to."
