Running a $2M–$150M business as its CEO is one of the most demanding leadership positions there is. You're responsible for strategy, culture, performance, and the wellbeing of everyone who depends on the company.

And yet:

  • Most CEOs have coaches who help with mindset — but not business value
  • Most have advisors who help with finance — but not leadership
  • Almost none have someone who connects both — who understands that how you lead directly affects what your business is worth

The result: CEOs making decisions in isolation, with no structured advisory relationship that operates where leadership development and business value actually intersect.

The Solution: CEO Coaching That's Wired Into Business Value From Day One

Your Unfiltered Value Proposition

Most CEO coaching helps you feel better about leading. Ours makes the business worth more because of how you lead. There's a direct line between your leadership capability, your team's depth, your operational structure, and your company's valuation. We work that line — all of it, not just the comfortable parts.

The "Hot Seat" Reality Check

Before any coaching begins, we put the business through a direct, unfiltered diagnostic. No soft questions. No feel-good frameworks. We look at what the business actually looks like from the outside — to a buyer, a successor, or a board — and we tell you exactly what we see.

This means pulling on the real numbers: financial audits, market analysis, competitor benchmarking, and leadership reviews that surface where the gaps are, what they're costing you, and what it would take to close them. Most CEOs find out things in this step they've been avoiding for years.

The "CEO-Only" Assessment

This is a private leadership assessment built specifically for the CEO — not the team, not the company. We assess your decision-making patterns, your leadership blind spots, your relationship to authority and ownership, and the specific ways your leadership is either accelerating or capping the business's value.

It's direct. It's uncomfortable in the right places. And it gives you a clear picture of exactly what the business needs from you right now — and what it needs you to stop doing.

The "3 Pillars" Focus Framework

Once the assessment is complete, all coaching work is organized around three pillars:

Pillar 1 — You

Your personal leadership effectiveness, decision-making structure, and the clarity you bring (or don't bring) to the organization.

Pillar 2 — Your Team

The executive team's capability, alignment, and ability to operate without your direct involvement in every decision.

Pillar 3 — Your Business

The operational systems, organizational design, and value drivers that make the business transferable, scalable, and worth what you think it is.

Every session, every engagement, every deliverable maps back to one of these three pillars — so nothing is ever just coaching for its own sake.
